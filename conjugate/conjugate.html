Basic French Conjugations


Conjugating French Verbs

  • Just like the English language, there are also verbs in French. However, the way these verbs are said and wrote are different. In French, verbs need to be conjugated, or changed according to whoever is speaking.
  • This website makes it easy to understand basic conjugations for French.
  • In French, there are two types of verbs, regular verbs and irregular verbs. The two differ on the way that they get conjugated.
  • Each of these verbs can also be subdivided into further categories
  • Click to expand on each accordion to learn more.

    • IMPORTANT: This website doesn't show the conjugations in the past or future tense, only in the present for now.

    Regular verbs


    Format for regular -er verbs
    Je -e Nous -ons
    Tu -es Vous -ez
    Il/Elle/On -e Ils/Elles -ent

    EXCEPTION: When a verb in its infinitive form and has the root -ger (such as voyager), then the conjugation for nous will be -eons.

    Regular -er verbs can be divided further into stem-changing verbs in which the conjugation is the same, but the accents are different based on if it is "inside/outside the boot".

    In this case, the verbs outside the boot will have the same accent as the infinitive while those inside will have different accents.

    The "boot" on the stem-changing verbs have been highlighted on this website to make it easy to identify.



    Format for regular -ir verbs
    Je -is Nous -issons
    Tu -is Vous -issez
    Il/Elle/On -it Ils/Elles -issent



    Format for regular -re verbs
    Je -s Nous -ons
    Tu -is Vous -ez
    Il/Elle/On -() Ils/Elles -ent




    Irregular verbs

    Unlike regular verbs, some irregular verbs follow a pattern while others don't.

    There are two main groups that can be conjugated in the irregular verbs. The first group is a seperate conjugation, but the second group is conjugated exactly like how you conjugate regular -er verbs.


    The first group
    Je -s Nous -ons
    Tu -s Vous -ez
    Il/Elle/On -t Ils/Elles -ent



    The second group
    Je -e Nous -ons
    Tu -es Vous -ez
    Il/Elle/On -e Ils/Elles -ent


    The rest of the irregular verbs don't have a specific way of conjugating, so it is essential for you to memorize it!
